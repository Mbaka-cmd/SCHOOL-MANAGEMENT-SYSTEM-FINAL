Our Curriculum

Chuka Girls Secondary School follows Kenya's national secondary school curriculum as set by the Kenya Institute of Curriculum Development (KICD) and examined by the Kenya National Examinations Council (KNEC) through the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE).

Students in Forms 1 and 2 take a broad set of compulsory subjects to build a strong academic foundation. In Forms 3 and 4, students select elective subjects based on their chosen stream - Sciences or Humanities - to specialise and prepare for KCSE examinations held every November.

The school is also progressively adapting to the new Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) framework as directed by the Ministry of Education.

Subject Streams

Students choose their stream at the start of Form 3

Sciences Stream

For students with a strong foundation in Mathematics and the natural sciences. Prepares students for Medicine, Engineering, Agriculture, and Applied Sciences at university.

  • Mathematics (compulsory)
  • Biology
  • Chemistry
  • Physics
  • English & Kiswahili (compulsory)
  • Agriculture or Computer Studies (elective)

Humanities Stream

For students with a passion for languages, social sciences, and business. Prepares students for Law, Education, Business, Arts, and Social Sciences at university.

  • English & Kiswahili (compulsory)
  • History & Government
  • Geography
  • Christian Religious Education
  • Business Studies
  • Home Science or Fasihi ya Kiswahili (elective)

Academic Support Programs

We go beyond the classroom to ensure every student succeeds

Evening Preps

Supervised study sessions every evening to reinforce classroom learning and complete assignments.

Mock Examinations

Form 4 students sit mock exams in October to prepare thoroughly for the KCSE national examinations in November.

Career Guidance

Regular career seminars, mentorship sessions, and counselling to help students make informed academic and career choices.

Science Exhibitions

Annual internal science fairs and participation in regional competitions to foster innovation and research skills.
