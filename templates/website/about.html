Our Story

Chuka Girls Secondary School, officially known as St. Bakhita Chuka Girls High School, is a government-run Extra County girls boarding school located along Kiagondu Forest Road, Karingari, Chuka - in the heart of Tharaka-Nithi County, Kenya.

Named after Saint Josephine Bakhita - a symbol of resilience, faith, and grace - the school has long served as a foundation for young women from across the Eastern Region and beyond. Operating under the Ministry of Education and the Teachers Service Commission, the school offers Forms 1 through 4 under Kenya's national KCSE curriculum.

With over 500 students, more than 30 qualified TSC-registered teachers, and 16 streams across four forms, we are committed to nurturing capable, disciplined, and morally grounded young women. Our alumni include some of Kenya's most distinguished public servants, among them Chief Justice Martha Koome - the first woman to hold that office in the Republic of Kenya.

Notable Alumni

Hon. Martha Koome

Chief Justice of Kenya

The 15th Chief Justice of the Republic of Kenya and the first woman to hold the office. A proud alumna of St. Bakhita Chuka Girls High School, she visited the school in 2019 and continues to be an inspiration to every student who walks through our gates.
